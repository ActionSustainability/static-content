THE SUSTAINABILITY DATA ​CAPTURE TOOL (SDCT)

SSEN Transmission contractors are invited to report sustainability data by creating a free account. Once registered and logged in, the following is required:

For each project:

  • An embodied carbon estimate report for designs at the beginning of each project [download template here]
  • An annual 'as built' embodied carbon report [download template here]. This allows the performance of projects to be monitored as the SDCT converts this performance into carbon emissions.
  • A monthly environmental report [download question set here and the waste log here]
  • A monthly social value report [download question set here]

For your organisation:

  • An annual company survey [download question set here]​

The sustainability data reported will be visible , in real-time, at a project level. Dashboards will communicate performance and will be utilised to measure targets and track progress.

At the business unit and corporate level, SSEN Transmission will review the performance of each organisation in the supply chain and as a collective. Targets are set by SSEN Transmission to encourage collaboration and achieve SSE’s business goals for 2030.​

HELPING SSE REACH​ THEIR 2030 GOALS​

SSE plc is a UK-listed energy company that operates throughout the UK and Ireland. It is involved principally in the generation, transmission and distribution of electricity; and, in the supply of energy and related services to customers.

SSE has set four fundamental goals for 2030, directly linked to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, which puts sustainability at the heart of SSE’s business strategy. The aim is to share the economic, social, and environmental opportunities created by SSE as they reach their 2030 Goals.

Importantly, the Sustainable Procurement Code outlines the expectations and requirements on SSE’s suppliers and contractors, and their supply chains. To ensure that SSE’s business is conducted ethically, sustainably and within the law, SSE expects its suppliers to meet the principles outlined in the Code and Guidance. This means as a minimum complying with local laws and regulations and respecting internationally recognised human and labour rights, however the values of fairness and respect are equally important to SSE. You can see the full Sustainable Procurement Code here and guidance for suppliers here.
